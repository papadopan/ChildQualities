Analytical Trail :

Most of the data came from the website "World Values Journey" (http://www.worldvaluessurvey.org/wvs.jsp), which is a global network of scientists who study the changing values and the impact in the humanity.

This study consists all the waves (1981 - 2014) , every country from every wave (#87), and is very focused to which child qualities, the parents considered as the most important (Good manners, Independence , Hard Work, etc.).

Another data source, was the Gapminder website, which provided the number of child deaths for every country

Therefore the above visualization presents, how important for every country for a specific year these child qualities are and also presents the number of child deaths for this year.

The users can select the quality and the year that want and then visualize them

The users also can select if they want to zoom in or zoom out to the data

Size of the circles: the importance of the quality

Color of circles: Europe(Red),Asia(yellow),America (blue),Africa (green),Oceania(purple)

Horizontal position of the circles: number of child deaths for the specific year

Research question :

The variety of parent's opinion about the most important child qualities, and how these opinions reflect to the number of child deaths

Valuable Insights:

  • India is the country with the biggest number of child deaths across the years
  • The most important value in China and in India is: Hard Work
  • Contries from Africa consider good manners as more important child quality than the hard work
  • During years Sweden has the least child deaths, and hard work as the least child quality
  • Mostly countries from America or Asia think "religious faith" as very important quality
  • Imagination rates are very low especially before 2000
  • Nigeria is the country with the highest rate of good manners
  • India has the most deaths and consider the hard work as most important during the years, while Sweden with the least deaths consider the Hard work as the least important
